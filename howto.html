Howto

Teams

The most important element of any competition is the team. Here 'team' is used generically to mean a team, club, individual competitor, or any other entity that takes part in the competition.

Start typing names into the 'add a team' textbox. When you press enter, it will be added to the list of teams below.

The order in which you enter teams is not important as they can be reordered later. However, the entered order is by default the order of seeding if using a DE format.

You can drag teams in the list up or down to change the order. This will automatically cause the schedule to be recalculated

Click on the '×' on any team to remove it from the list.

Headers

You can add a title and subtitle to your competition. These will appear at the top of the output schedule.

Formats

The second most important element is the format of the competition. Currently the tool supports two main formats, each with a number of sub-formats.

Round robin

The first format is round robin, where every team plays every other team in the competition. The tool calculates the number of rounds required for a round robin, and then assigns matches between two teams for each round. If there is an odd number of teams, then one team will have a 'rest round' where they don't play a match. Given n teams, there will be n rounds if n is odd, and n-1 if n is even.

Direct Elimination

The other main format is direct elimination, or knock-out. Each team is matched with another, and the winner progresses to the next round. If the number of competitors is not a power of 2, then a number of teams will receive 'byes' from that round of matches.

The order in which the teams are ordered in the list will determine the seedings, the team at the top of the list being the number 1 seed. By default, the matches will be created so that the top seed plays the bottom seed, the second seed plays second-to-bottom and so on. Other options are to completely randomise the matchups regardless of seeding, or to seed the first two teams only.
